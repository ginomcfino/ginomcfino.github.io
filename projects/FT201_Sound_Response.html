Expulsion from the Garden of Eden

Thomas Cole (1801 - 1848)

Project Description:

To me, the story of Adam and Eve represents a story about the growth of humanity.

In case you want to learn more about this piece, here's what the museum has to say:

"In the biblical story from Genesis, Adam and Eve -- having displeased the Lord by eating the forbidden fruit that gave them knowledge of good and evil -- were expelled from Eden ...

Rather than describing their anguish through shameful poses and distraught expressions, as artists since the Renaissance had done, Cole told the story through the landscape: he contrasts Paradise, lush and topical, with the harsh, violent, external world.

Adam and Eve seem propelled from Eden by a shaft of light that represents God's power and are seemingly swalled up by a shaft of light that represents God's power and are seemingly swallowed up by the forbidding landscape beyond."

- Museum of Fine Arts (Boston)
